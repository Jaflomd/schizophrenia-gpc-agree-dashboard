## Needs manual read

- REC-0537 (low_source_overlap_0.22): For treatment-resistant schizophrenia when clozapine is not used, concomitant therapy with antipsychotics other than clozapine and electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) may improve psychiatric symptoms in the short term and reduce relapse in the short-to-medium term. Because possible deterioration of cognitive function has been shown, ECT with non-clozapine antipsychotics should be implemented solely when clozapine use presents considerable challenges. Evidence is insufficient to support ECT without antipsychotics, and therefore it is not recommended.
